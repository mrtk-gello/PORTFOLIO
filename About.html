This portfolio was first and foremost created as a major requirement for the Human Computer Interaction subject.
But more than that, it became a digital archive of my hard work, growth, and creativity.

Personal Projects

Behind the Scenes

The Web Design project for our eCommerce assignment was one of the most intense parts of the semester. With a tight deadline and barely any progress, I decided to pull an all-nighter at a classmate’s house—though I only managed an hour of sleep. The task was individual, but I still found myself fueled by coffee, snacks, and determination. I spent the night debugging, designing, and pushing through exhaustion. Although the website didn't fully function yet, I was able to meet the deadline. Despite the chaos, it was a great learning experience, and I was proud of how much I accomplished on my own.

For our final project, which was creating a personal website portfolio, the journey was just as challenging. The video shows snippets of the code behind the website, still a work in progress as I continue editing and refining it. The sleepless nights and constant revisions have been overwhelming, but they’ve been necessary to get closer to the vision I have for this portfolio. Every line of code, every tweak in design has been a step toward something I can be proud of. Although it’s not finished yet, I’m hopeful that with continued effort, it will bloom into a meaningful representation of my skills and growth.

This video showcases my very first web design project, which was purely a mockup website. As a beginner in web design, I had little experience with coding but had a background in other design work, which helped me create the layout and structure. Despite being new to web design, I managed to complete the mockup ahead of the deadline and presented it with confidence. The hard work paid off when I received a perfect score for the project. It was a moment of personal achievement, marking the beginning of my journey into the world of web design.
